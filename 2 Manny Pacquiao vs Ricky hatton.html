#2: Manny Pacquiao vs. Ricky Hatton

Justin Constance SD. Espejo

The Hatton Training Camp

Ricky Hatton(left) shadowboxing as Head Coach, Floyd "Joy" Mayweather Sr.(right) watches his fighter


Coming from a loss from Floyd "Money" Mayweather, at this point in his career, Ricky

"The Hitman" Hatton is looking for a big comeback fight. Hiring his conqueror's

father and trainer, the new duo sought to beat the pound for pound best boxer in the

world at this time: Manny Pacquiao. Just like the Filipino, Hatton is an offensive

boxer that puts emphasis on body punching to knock out his opponents and make them

quit. His entire career is full of these kinds of knockouts and this is what Ricky

trained to deliver against Manny Pacquiao. Mayweather Sr. also added more defense

to Ricky's offensive arsenal, emphasizing the danger in Pacquiao's left hand.

Mayweather Sr. Seeks to do this with the aid of his "Philly" shell defensive techniques.

Ricky Hatton trains with new Head Coach, Floyd "Joy" Mayweather in his preparation to fight champion Manny Pacquiao.


The Pacquiao Training Camp

Manny Pacquiao hits the double-end bag to practice speed and timing for his fight with Ricky Hatton.


Coming from a devastating TKO victory from Oscar De la Hoya, Pacquiao is now set to

train against Ricky "The Hitman" Hatton in this light-welterweight title fight.

To arm Manny for this fight, Freddie roach came up with a brilliant new technique

that he called "The Manila Ice" a short, timed right hook which can vary in power

depending on how it is thrown. This right hook is made to create an opening for

the filipino's left hand which is his power shot. In training camp, speed and

timing are the keys to victory for Pacquiao.
