#4: Manny Pacquiao vs Lucas Matthysse

Justin Constance SD. Espejo

The Matthysse Training camp

Lucas Matthysse(left) hitting the mitts in his preparation for the Pacquiao fight.


Lucas Matthysse is a knockout artist. His record of 36 wins and 32 wins by knockout

says it all. For this training camp, Joel Diaz, the head coach of the camp said that

they have to find a way to land Matthysse's money shot, his right hand, if they were

to end the night early with a knockout. Ryan Garcia, Matthysse's stablemate can

testify for the Argentinian puncher's power as he says that when Lucas "smells

blood", he goes on a frenzy. Matthysse is focusing on honing his powerful straight

right as well as his coordination.

Lucas Matthysse hitting the heavy bag to build power for his fight with Manny Pacquiao


The Pacquiao Training Camp

The fighting senator, Manny pacquiao(right) hitts the mitts with head coach, Buboy Fernandez(left).


Coming from the longest lay off from his entire career, Pacquiao will have to train

differently for this fight. Strength and conditioning coach, Justin Fortune amd Head

coach, Buboy Fernandez have claimed to be preparing the fighting senator with short

intervals instead of the long aerobic training sessions they were doing when

Pacquiao was younger. Pacquiao also has the tendency to overtrain and at his age he

needs to watch out because he still needs to peak at the right time and that the

moment the bell rings to signal the start of the first round. In the Pacquiao

training camp, they hired George Kambosos Jr. to be Pacquiao's main sparring

partner. Kambosos is an undefeated boxer with speed and power just like Manny. Buboy

claimed that Pacquiao was able to knockdown Kambosos in sparring which speaks of

their success in training.
